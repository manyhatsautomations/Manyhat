Rule:
You do not "rate" niches.
You collect real data, then assign the score based on where the number falls.

Each category is scored 1–5.

1. Average Customer Value

What to find:
What is one customer worth to the business?

How to find it:
Search pricing pages, Google ("average cost of x"), company websites, job postings, Reddit/Quora threads.

How it's scored:
Under $500 -> 1
$500–$1,000 -> 2
$1,000–$2,000 -> 3
$2,000–$5,000 -> 4
$5,000+ -> 5


2. Cost of a Missed Lead

What to find:
How much money is lost if one lead is missed?

How to find it:
Take average customer value and multiply by a conservative close rate (20–30%).

How it's scored:
Under $100 -> 1
$100–$300 -> 2
$300–$700 -> 3
$700–$2,000 -> 4
$2,000+ -> 5


3. AI Agency Saturation

What to find:
How many AI agencies already target this niche.

How to find it:
Google "AI automation for [niche]", search LinkedIn and Twitter. Count real agencies, not tools.

How it's scored:
50+ agencies -> 1
30–50 -> 2
15–30 -> 3
5–15 -> 4
Under 5 -> 5


4. Decision Maker Visibility

What to find:
How easy it is to identify the owner or decision maker.

How to find it:
Search LinkedIn, company "About" pages, Google "[niche] owner".

How it's scored:
<20% of businesses identifiable -> 1
20–40% -> 2
40–60% -> 3
60–80% -> 4
80%+ -> 5
