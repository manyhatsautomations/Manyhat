## COLD CALLING APPROACH

Paul is a complete beginner at cold calling. He struggles most with the opener.

**Primary opener (Script 0):**
> "Hey [Name], the reason I'm calling is I was doing a business project about booking more appointments and having less people go to competitors. By your Google reviews I can see you're already doing great, so I'm not even sure if it would make sense for you — but do you sometimes miss calls or take longer to get back to texts?"

**Key principles:**
- Sound casual, not scripted
- "Not even sure if this makes sense for you" disarms the prospect
- Lead with their problem, not the product
- Ask questions — don't pitch
- Goal of every call = send them the demo link (not to close)
- The demo closes the deal, Paul just gets them there

## OBJECTION HANDLING (KEY ONES)

**"We already have an answering service"**
→ "Does it handle texts too? Because most leads are texting first now."

**"We tried a chatbot, it was terrible"**
→ "Most chatbots ARE terrible — generic templates. This is trained on YOUR specific business. Try the demo — 2 minutes."

**"How much does it cost?"** (asked early)
→ "Depends on what you need. First — what happens to your leads after hours? The price looks different depending on how much you're already losing."

**"Just send me an email"**
→ "Happy to. But honestly the demo shows it better than any email. What's your cell? I'll text it — 2 minutes on your phone."

**"Not interested"**
→ "Fair. Quick question — when a lead calls while your guys are on a job, what happens?"

**"I need to think about it"**
→ "Of course. How many leads do you get a month? Because every one between now and then is a chance for a competitor to respond first."

**"Can't afford it"**
→ "We have payment plans and start at $3K setup + $99/month. For most businesses it pays for itself with 1-2 extra jobs a month. Try the demo first."
