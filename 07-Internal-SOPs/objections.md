# Objections & Rebuttals — Full Reference

## Framework for Every Objection
1. **Acknowledge** — "I totally get that" / "That makes sense" / "Fair"
2. **Reframe** — Connect their objection to their pain
3. **Bridge to demo** — Keep it low commitment

Never argue. Never repeat yourself. Always acknowledge first.

---

## COMMON COLD CALL OBJECTIONS

### "We already have someone handling our calls / answering service"
> "Yeah that's actually what most of my clients had before. Quick question — does your answering service respond to texts as well? Or just calls? [pause] Because a lot of the leads coming in now are texting first, especially from Google."

### "I'm not interested"
> "Totally fair. Just out of curiosity — when a new lead calls while your guys are on a job, what happens? [pause] Does it go to voicemail or does someone always pick up?"

### "We tried a chatbot before and it didn't work"
> "Ugh, I've heard that a lot. Most chatbots ARE terrible — they're generic and robotic. This is different because it's trained specifically on YOUR business, not a template. But don't take my word for it — try the demo, takes 2 minutes. If it doesn't blow that other thing out of the water, I'll leave you alone."

### "How much does it cost?" (asked too early)
> "I'll be totally upfront on that — depends on what you need. But before I throw numbers at you, can I ask — right now, when a lead texts or calls after hours, what happens? [pause] Because the cost is pretty different depending on how much business you're already losing."

### "Just send me an email"
> "Happy to! Honestly though, a PDF won't show you what this actually does. What's your cell? I'll text you the demo — it takes 2 minutes on your phone and you'll see it better than any email could explain. Then I'll send you the email too."

### "I'm too busy right now"
> "I get it. That's actually why I'm calling — the whole point is this saves you time by handling the follow-up automatically. Here's what I'll do: I'll text you the demo link right now. You try it when you have 2 minutes — could be tonight, on your phone. No meetings. Sound fair?"

### "Let me think about it / not ready yet"
> "Of course. Quick question though — how many leads do you typically get in a month? Because every one of those between now and when you're ready is a chance for a competitor to respond first. What if we got you set up now so you're not leaving money on the table?"

### "I need to talk to my business partner"
> "Makes sense. Would it help if I put together a quick overview they could look at? Or even better — want to loop them in on a 10-minute call this week? I'll answer their questions directly. Makes it easier."

### "I can't afford that"
> "I hear you — budget always matters. We do have payment plans and you can start with the entry-level and upgrade when it's paying for itself. The setup starts at $3,000 and after that it's $99 a month — for most businesses it pays for itself with one or two extra jobs a month. Would it help to try the demo first so you can see if it's even worth the conversation?"

### "I already respond fast to my leads"
> "That's awesome — you're ahead of most. Out of curiosity, what about texts and calls that come in after hours or on weekends? Does someone handle those same day or is it the next morning?"

### "I'm not a tech person"
> "Neither are most of my clients — that's why we handle everything. You don't touch any of it. We build it, we set it up, we maintain it. You just show up to the appointments it books for you."

### "My customers prefer talking to a real person"
> "Totally get that. It's not replacing you — it's handling the initial response and booking so you can focus on the actual job. Think of it like a receptionist that works 24/7 and never misses a message. Your clients still talk to you — this just makes sure none of them fall through the cracks at 10pm."

### "I don't see how this would work for my business"
> "Fair — every business is a little different. That's exactly why I built a demo — takes 2 minutes, you try it yourself, and you'll see how it would work for [their specific type of business]. What's your cell? I'll send it right now."

## WHEN THE PROSPECT GOES SILENT
Give them 3-4 seconds. Then:
> "Still there?"
If more silence:
> "Happy to send you the demo link and let you try it at your own pace — no pressure either way."

---

## WHEN THEY KEEP DEFLECTING
After 2 deflections, go direct:
> "[Name], I can tell you're not sold on the idea yet — totally fair. Here's the honest truth: I can't prove this in a 5-minute call. The demo does that way better than I can. 2 minutes on your phone. If it doesn't impress you, you never hear from me again. Fair enough?"
