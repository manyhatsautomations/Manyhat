# Paul's Cold Calling Scripts — Reference

## The Philosophy
- Sound curious, not salesy
- "I'm not even sure if this makes sense for you" = disarms guard
- Lead with THEIR problem — not your product
- Goal of every call = get them to the 2-minute demo, NOT to close
- The demo does the selling, you just get them there

---

## PRIMARY OPENER (Script 0 — Recommended for Paul)

**If they have a website:**
> "Hey [Name], the reason I'm calling is because I was doing a business project about booking more appointments and having less people go to competitors. By your Google reviews I can see you're already doing great, so I'm not even sure if it would make sense for you — but do you sometimes miss calls or take longer to get back to texts?"

**They say YES:**
> "Yeah it's normal. I actually made a receptionist based on your business as a project — it makes sure everyone who's interested gets their questions answered and is booked in immediately. It turned out pretty good which is why I decided to call you. Would something like that even help you?"

**They say NO:**
> "That's awesome. Doesn't that take a lot of time away from work if you're [e.g., 'on a job site']? Or do you just not get a lot of calls?"

If they spend time on it:
> "Yeah, sounds like it'd take up hours during the week. Would it help if you had something like a receptionist to handle that?"

If they don't get many calls:
> "I see. If I could make sure more people were calling and needing your services, would that help?"

---

## OPENER VARIATIONS

**Script 2 — CEO to CEO (consultative):**
> "[Name], I'm sorry — this is Paul. I don't normally make calls about this but our team told me about your company. We help [industry] businesses get more jobs from leads they're already missing. Do you have a lot of calls or texts that don't get answered right away?"

**Script 4 — Pattern interrupt:**
> "Hi [Name], we don't know each other — if I mention this is a cold call, would that ruin your day? I work with [industry] businesses to help them respond to every lead in seconds so they stop losing jobs to whoever picks up first."

---

## PIVOTING TO THE DEMO

Once they've shown ANY interest:
> "I actually built a demo of this specifically for [their industry] businesses — it literally takes 2 minutes to try. Want me to send it to your cell right now? No sign-up, nothing — just text the demo and you'll see exactly how it works."

If they hesitate:
> "I'm not asking you to buy anything. Two minutes on your phone, try it, and if it's not impressive I'll leave you alone."

---

## WHAT "GOOD" LOOKS LIKE

A good call from Paul:
1. Opener sounds natural (not read from a script)
2. First question is about THEIR business, not a pitch
3. "Not sure if this makes sense for you" framing included
4. Prospect says yes/no → Paul acknowledges then bridges
5. Objection comes → Paul acknowledges FIRST ("totally get that") → brief reframe → pivot to demo
6. Call ends within 3-4 minutes with demo link sent or appointment set

Red flags:
- Talking for 20+ seconds without asking a question
- Starting with "I'm calling about our AI product..."
- Explaining how AI works before knowing if they have a problem
- Repeating the same point after an objection
- Sounding like he's reading

---

## THE DEMO CLOSE

The last line of every successful call:
> "Awesome — what's the best cell to text the demo to? Takes 2 minutes and I'll follow up tomorrow to see what you thought."

OR if they want to talk more first:
> "Want to hop on a 10-minute call this week? I'll show you exactly how it would work for your business specifically."
