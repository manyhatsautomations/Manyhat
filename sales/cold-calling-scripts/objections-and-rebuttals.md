# Objections & Rebuttals

## Source: "What Do I Say When..." Guide

---

### "Things are good, pretty busy right now" (Lukewarm)
> "Good to hear — busy is better than slow, right? Hey quick question: when a new lead comes in, how quickly are you guys able to follow up? Same day, or does it sometimes take longer?"

---

### "What exactly are you selling?" / "What is this?"
> "Fair question! I'm doing a private beta for an AI assistant that responds to your leads instantly — like within 60 seconds, 24/7. I actually built a demo customized to your business so you can see exactly how it would work. Takes 2 minutes to try. Want me to send it over?"

---

### "Can you do any better on the price?"
> "I hear you. Here's the thing — the setup takes real time to do right, and we don't cut corners. What I CAN do is [offer payment plan / extended trial / bonus feature]. Would that help?"

---

### "Let me revisit this in January / next month / next quarter"
> "Totally understand — timing matters. Quick question though: how many leads do you typically get between now and then? Because every one of those is a chance for a competitor to respond first. What if we got you set up now so you're not leaving money on the table?"

---

### "I need to talk to my business partner / spouse"
> "Of course — smart to get alignment. Would it help if I put together a quick summary they can look at? Or even better, want to loop them into a quick call so I can answer their questions directly? I'm happy to make it easy."

---

### "We tried a chatbot before and it was terrible"
> "Ugh, I've heard that a lot actually. Most chatbots ARE terrible — they're generic and robotic. This is different because we train it specifically on YOUR business. But don't take my word for it — try the demo. 2 minutes. If it's not way better than what you tried before, I'll leave you alone."

---

### "I saw something similar for $99/month"
> "Yeah, there's a lot of cheap options out there. Here's the difference: those are template bots you set up yourself. We custom-build this for your business, integrate it with your CRM, and optimize it until it's actually converting. You get what you pay for. The question is — do you want cheap, or do you want results?"

---

### "I literally have no time for this"
> "I totally get it — that's actually WHY you need this. The whole point is it saves you time by handling lead response automatically. Here's what I can do: I'll send you the demo link, you try it when you have 2 minutes. No meetings needed. What's your cell? I'll text it over."

---

### "Just send me an email with the details"
> "Happy to! But honestly, a PDF won't show you what this actually does. The demo takes 2 minutes and you'll understand it better than any email could explain. What's your cell? I'll text it to you and you can try it whenever. Then if you have questions, I'll follow up with that email."

---

### They ghost after the demo
> "Hey [Name]! Saw you checked out the demo — what did you think? Any questions, or anything that seemed off? Either way no pressure — just want to make sure you got value from it."

---

### They ghost after the VSL / video
> "Hey [Name]! Did the walkthrough video make sense? Happy to jump on a quick call if anything was confusing, or just answer questions over text if that's easier. What works best for you?"

---

### They go silent after showing interest
> "Hey [Name]! Just circling back — did you get a chance to check out the demo? No pressure either way, just want to make sure it didn't get buried. Let me know if you have questions!"

---

## General Objection Framework

1. **Acknowledge** — "I hear you" / "Totally understand"
2. **Reframe** — Connect their objection back to their pain
3. **Bridge to demo** — The demo closes, not you
4. **Low-commitment ask** — "2 minutes on your phone" / "I'll text it to you"

> 💡 Remember: The demo does the selling. Your job is just to get it in front of them.
