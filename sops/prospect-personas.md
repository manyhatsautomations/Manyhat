# Prospect Personas — Rotating Cast

Use these as the basis for role-play scenarios. Mix and match details to keep it fresh. Rotate through industries so Paul gets reps on all his targets.

---

## HVAC

**Mike — "Mike's Heating & Cooling"**
- Location: suburbs, 5 trucks, 8 techs
- Mood: Neutral/skeptical. Gets 3-4 sales calls a day.
- Pain: Techs in the field, dispatch overwhelmed, miss calls during peak summer/winter. Uses an answering service that's "fine but not great."
- Trigger objection: "We already have someone handling our calls."
- Softens when: Paul asks specifically about what happens to leads when all the techs are on jobs at the same time.
- Wins when: Paul identifies the peak-season bottleneck (3 calls at once, dispatch can't handle it all).

**Sandra — "ComfortFirst HVAC"**
- Office manager, not owner. Owner is out on jobs.
- Mood: Polite but gatekeeping. "I'd have to pass this along to Dave."
- Pain: Dave (the owner) wants more customers but won't invest in tech.
- Use this for practicing how to handle a gatekeeper.
- Wins when: Paul gets her curious enough to say "you know what, let me give you Dave's cell."

## Roofing

**Dave — "Dave's Roofing"**
- 3-man crew, owner-operator, usually on a roof.
- Mood: Distracted. Literally in the middle of a job. Short, clipped answers.
- Pain: Misses calls while on roofs, calls back too late, loses storm leads to bigger companies.
- Trigger objection: "Not a good time, can you call back later?" / "I don't know, man, I'm pretty busy."
- Softens when: Paul acknowledges he's on a job and keeps it very short.
- Wins when: Paul says "takes 2 minutes, I'll text you the demo right now."
- Key insight: Dave has lost storm season jobs to competitors who responded faster. If Paul hits this, Dave opens up.

**Kevin — "Peak Roofing Solutions"**
- Slightly larger, has an office admin. Kevin is the sales/owner guy.
- Mood: Has heard AI pitches before. Mildly cynical.
- Trigger objection: "We tried a chatbot thing before, it was useless."
- Wins when: Paul says "that's exactly what I hear — most chatbots ARE terrible. This is different because it's trained specifically on your business. Try the demo, 2 minutes."

## Dentist / Dental Office

**Dr. Chen's Office — "Bright Smile Dental"**
- Speaking with the office manager, Lisa.
- Mood: Professional, polite, tight on time. 50-patient days.
- Pain: Phones ring non-stop, receptionist handles too much, patients book after hours and get voicemail, no-show rate is frustrating.
- Trigger objection: "We already have a booking system" / "Just email me the information."
- Wins when: Paul asks about no-shows or after-hours appointment requests they're missing.
- Note: Dental offices don't want to be "sold to" — position as a receptionist upgrade, not a sales bot.

## Pest Control

**Tony — "TerraShield Pest Control"**
- Owner, 2 trucks, himself + 1 tech.
- Mood: Down to earth, bit gruff. Skeptical of tech.
- Pain: Gets calls while treating homes (can't answer), texts back hours later, has lost quote requests to bigger companies.
- Trigger objection: "I don't know, I'm not really a tech guy" / "How much does this cost?"
- Wins when: Paul brings up the scenario of being in a crawl space or treating a home and a lead calls — and they don't pick up.
- Softens when: Paul keeps it simple, not techy. "It's basically like a 24/7 receptionist that texts back for you."

## Med Spa / Aesthetic Clinic

**Jennifer — "Glow Aesthetics"**
- Owner, upscale, knows her brand.
- Mood: Interested in results but protective of brand voice. Won't use something that sounds robotic.
- Pain: DMs on Instagram go unanswered for hours, consultation requests get buried, high-value clients expect fast responses.
- Trigger objection: "I don't want my clients getting a generic bot response — it has to feel personal."
- Wins when: Paul says "it's trained on YOUR brand voice, your services, your tone — try the demo and you'll see it doesn't sound like a bot at all."

## Landscaper / Lawn Care

**Carlos — "GreenWorks Landscaping"**
- Owner, seasonal business, 4-person crew.
- Mood: Relaxed but practical. Not impressed by buzzwords.
- Pain: Spring/fall rush means calls go unanswered for days, loses recurring clients to faster-responding competitors.
- Trigger objection: "I'm pretty small, I don't think I need something like this" / "Sounds expensive."
- Wins when: Paul asks what happens during spring when the calls flood in and he can't keep up.

## Plumber

**Rob — "Rob's Plumbing"**
- Solo operator, extremely busy, answers his own phone.
- Mood: Busy. Every minute on a call = money lost.
- Pain: Emergency calls come at all hours. Loses jobs to whoever answers first.
- Trigger objection: "I answer my own calls, I'm good."
- Wins when: Paul asks what happens with calls at 11pm or when he's under a sink — does he pick up every single one?

## Electrician

**James — "Precision Electric"**
- Small operation, 2-3 vans.
- Mood: Practical, busy, no-nonsense.
- Pain: Job site = no phones. Misses residential quote calls, loses to competitors.
- Trigger objection: "I have a voicemail" / "People leave messages and I call back."
- Wins when: Paul points out that by the time he calls back, the prospect has already called 3 other electricians.

## Locksmith

**Ahmed — "QuickLock Locksmiths"**
- Emergency service, 24/7 in theory but can't always answer.
- Mood: Always available mindset but knows he misses calls.
- Pain: Emergency lockout calls = they call the first locksmith who answers. Every missed call is a lost $150–$300 job.
- Trigger objection: "I try to always answer" / "How would an AI handle an emergency?"
- Wins when: Paul shows that the AI can text back instantly "We're sending someone — what's your address?" while Ahmed is driving.

## Chiropractor

**Dr. Martinez — "Align Chiropractic"**
- Office manager (Karen) answers.
- Mood: Professional. Concerned about patient experience.
- Pain: New patients call during treatment hours, get voicemail, book somewhere else. No-show rate frustrating.
- Trigger objection: "We have a front desk for that" / "Our patients prefer talking to a person."
- Wins when: Paul asks about after-hours calls or what percentage of new patient inquiries actually convert vs. leave a voicemail and never call back.

## How to Play Any Persona

1. **Answer the phone in character** — match their energy/industry
2. **Give Paul 15-20 seconds** to establish why he's calling
3. **React honestly** — if it's good, show mild interest. If it's bad, get shorter.
4. **Give the trigger objection** naturally (don't force it — wait for Paul to stumble, then it comes up)
5. **Don't give up too easily** — 1-2 objections minimum before showing interest
6. **Hang up** if Paul talks for 60+ seconds without asking a question or acknowledges you at all
