# 5-Phase Cold Call Playbook — ManyHats Reference Card

Read this before every practice session. This is the complete structure.

---

## The 5 Phases

| Phase | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1. Opener | 0–15s | Pattern interrupt. Own the cold call. End with a question. Never pitch. |
| 2. Disarm | 15–30s | Remove all pressure. One question. Then shut up completely. |
| 3. Poke Pain | 30–60s | Make them say the pain in their own words. Never suggest the number. |
| 4. Demo Ask | 60–90s | Get them to try the 2-minute demo NOW at manyhatautomations.aidemo.app. |
| 5. Close | 90s+ | Book a specific time. Two-option close always. Never "does that work?" |

---

## Phase 1 — Opener (0–15s)

**Rule:** Pattern interrupt. Own that it's a cold call. Never pitch. End with a question about THEIR business.

Rotate through all 3 openers. Never use the same opener twice in a single session.

**Opener A — The Honest Interrupt**
> "Hey [Name] it's Paul. Look, I'll be straight — this is a cold call. I'll be shocked if you don't hang up. You want to give me 30 seconds or should I call back another time?"

**Opener B — The Curious Observer**
> "Hey [Name], the reason I'm calling — I was doing a project on [industry] businesses in the area. By your reviews you're already doing well, so honestly I'm not sure this makes sense for you. But do you ever miss calls or texts when you're on the job?"

**Opener C — The 27-Second Ask**
> "Hi [Name], I'm genuinely not sure this would make sense for you, but I had a quick thought about your business. Do you have 27 seconds?"

---

## Phase 2 — Disarm (15–30s)

**Rule:** Remove all pressure immediately. Say some version of "not sure this is even relevant for you yet." Ask ONE question. Then stop talking completely.

> "I'm honestly not sure this is even relevant for your situation — but I had one quick question."

Then ask the industry pain question (see below). Then shut up. Two-beat pause minimum.

---

## Phase 3 — Poke Pain (30–60s)

**Rule:** Make them say the pain in their own words. Ask how many jobs or leads per month they're losing. Never suggest a number — make them give you one.

- "Roughly how many calls do you think you miss in a week?"
- "When that happens — what does that cost you?"
- "Has that happened recently?"

If they say a number, reflect it back: "So if even 2 of those turned into jobs, what's that worth to you in a month?"

---

## Phase 4 — Demo Ask (60–90s)

**Rule:** No slides. No pitch deck. Get them to try the demo live on their phone right now.

> "Here's what I'd suggest — instead of me talking about it, can I just show you? Takes 2 minutes from your phone. Site's manyhatautomations.aidemo.app. You can pull it up right now."

If they hesitate:
> "It's not a form or a signup — it's a live demo. Literally just text it like a customer would and see what comes back."

**Demo closes. Paul does not close. Your job is to get them to the demo.**

---

## Phase 5 — Close

**Rule:** Book a specific time. Two-option close every time. Never say "does that work?" or "when are you free?"

> "I can walk you through the setup on Thursday morning or Friday afternoon — which is better for you?"

Then: morning or afternoon?

Then: nail down the exact time and confirm it.

---

## Industry Pain Questions (Phase 2)

Use the matching question for the persona. Deliver it, then go silent.

| Industry | Pain Hook Question |
|----------|--------------------|
| HVAC / Plumber / Electrician | "When someone calls at 7pm about an emergency — what happens if you miss that call?" |
| Roofer / Landscaper / Contractor | "When you get a quote request on the weekend — does that lead get followed up Monday or does it fall through?" |
| Dentist / Chiro / Med Spa | "How many appointment requests go unanswered because your front desk is with a patient — and then you never hear from that person again?" |
| Pest Control / Locksmith | "Someone searches for you at midnight — they call the first 3 numbers. What happens when yours rings and no one answers?" |

## Objection Rebuttals

Framework: **Acknowledge → Reframe → Bridge to demo** (Hormozi 3-A)

Never argue. Never defend. Acknowledge, then redirect.

---

**"We already have an answering service."**
> "Yeah — does your service respond to texts too? Because most leads are texting first now from Google."

---

**"Not interested."**
> "Totally fair. Just out of curiosity — when a call comes in while you're on a job, what happens?"

---

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "I'll be totally upfront — but price before you've seen it doesn't mean much. Can I show you the actual thing? 2 minutes from your phone."

---

**"We tried a chatbot before, it was useless."**
> "Ugh yeah, most chatbots are terrible. This is trained on YOUR business specifically. Try the demo, 2 minutes. If it's not completely different I'll leave you alone."

---

**"Just send me an email."**
> "Happy to. Honestly though, a PDF won't show you what this does. What's your cell? I'll text you the demo right now."

---

**"I answer my own calls, I'm good."**
> "That's great — what about texts at 11pm or when you're in the middle of a job?"

---

**"Not a good time."**
> "I get it. 30 seconds. Thursday morning or Friday afternoon — which works better?"

## Delivery Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Talk less than 40% | Listen more than you speak on every single call |
| End every turn with a question | Never make a statement and wait — always ask |
| 2-beat pause after every question | Never fill the silence. Count to two. |
| Never pitch features | Pitch the pain being solved, not the product |
| Demo closes | Your only job is to get them to manyhatautomations.aidemo.app |
