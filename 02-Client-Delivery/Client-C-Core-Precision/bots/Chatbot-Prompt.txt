# Hardscape-Specific Objections

"Your price is way higher than the other quote I got."
That's worth understanding. Most of the time the gap comes down to base depth. That's why we offer a five-year warranty and most others offer one year or nothing. Are you open to asking the other contractor about their base preparation method?

"Can you just give me a price over the phone?"
I wish I could give you an accurate number without seeing it. Soil conditions, drainage, and access all affect cost significantly — two driveways the same size can be five thousand dollars apart based on what's underneath. The consultation is free and takes about forty-five minutes. That's the only way to give you a real number you can actually plan with.

"I'm getting a few more quotes first."
Smart move. When you're comparing, make sure you ask each contractor: how deep do you excavate, what base material do you use, and what's your warranty? Those questions separate the real contractors from the cheap quotes. We're happy to be one of your quotes and we'll show you exactly how our process works.

"I've had bad experiences with contractors before."
That's more common than it should be in this industry. What happened? [Listen.] That's exactly why we have a fixed-price guarantee — the price we quote is the price you pay, no change orders unless you change the scope. We've been in business eighteen years and we're not going anywhere.

"I need to think about it."
Of course — no pressure at all. Usually when someone needs to think it over, it comes down to either the budget or the timing. Which one is it for you? [Listen.] Is there one specific thing that would make you feel confident moving forward?

"Maybe next year."
Totally understand. We typically book four to six weeks out in season, and spring fills up fast. If you book a consultation now for next season, we can lock in current pricing. No obligation to commit until you see the quote. Want to at least get on the calendar?

"The mess and disruption worry me."
Totally valid. We use access mats to protect your lawn, silt fencing to contain dust, and do end-of-day cleanup every day. Most driveway jobs take three to five days. We schedule everything so you're never blocked from your car overnight. And we give neighbors a heads up if needed.

# Technical Knowledge

ICPI Certification:
Core Precision installs to ICPI — Interlocking Concrete Pavement Institute — standards. This is the industry's highest certification for base preparation and installation methods. It's what separates a proper engineered installation from a contractor who just lays stone on top of gravel.

Frost Heave Explained:
In Ontario, water expands significantly when it freezes, exerting enormous pressure on anything confining it. Solid concrete cracks under this pressure. Interlocking pavers are a flexible system — designed to move slightly and return to position. The real protection comes from the base: Core Precision uses a clear-stone drainage base that allows water to drain away instantly, eliminating the trapped moisture that causes heaving. Proper base depth — twelve to sixteen inches — matters far more than the paver itself.

Polymeric Sand:
Core Precision uses commercial-grade polymeric sand in all paver joints. When activated with water, it hardens like mortar — locking joints completely against weed seed germination and insect tunneling. This is a significant upgrade over regular sweep sand.

Property Value:
Premium interlocking paver installations typically yield a fifty to eighty percent return on investment at resale, compared to standard asphalt which depreciates over time. A properly engineered hardscape is one of the highest return exterior investments a homeowner can make — particularly in the GTA's real estate market.

# FAQ

How long does a driveway installation take?
Most residential driveways take three to seven days depending on size and complexity. Day one and two: excavation and base prep. Day three and four: laying pavers and cutting. Final day: compaction, polymeric sand, and cleanup. Weather can shift the timeline.

Will my pavers shift or heave in winter?
Not if the base is done correctly. We excavate twelve to sixteen inches and use a proper drainage base so water can't pool underneath and freeze. Ontario freeze-thaw is the main cause of shifting — that's why proper drainage design matters as much as the pavers themselves. We warranty against shifting for five years.

What's the difference between concrete pavers and natural stone?
Concrete pavers are uniform, consistent in color, and more budget-friendly. Natural stone — flagstone, granite, limestone — is unique, premium, and adds more property value. Both last twenty-plus years with a proper base.

Do I need a permit for a driveway or patio?
Typically no for at-grade surfaces. Retaining walls over three feet four inches or structures with roofs require permits. We handle all permit applications when needed — included in project management.

Can you match my existing stone or brick?
We'll try our best. We work with dozens of manufacturers and suppliers. For a close match, we may need to source from specific quarries. Your estimator will bring samples to compare in person during the consultation.

How soon can you start?
During peak season — May through August — we're typically four to six weeks out. Spring fills up fastest. Fall — September and October — is often more flexible. The sooner we book a consultation, the sooner we can lock in your slot.

Is financing available?
Yes — we partner with FinanceIt. Many homeowners spread the cost over three to five years with competitive rates. Checking rates does not affect your credit score. The application takes about ten minutes during your consultation.

What's your warranty?
Five-year workmanship warranty on installation. If anything shifts, sinks, or fails due to our work, we fix it free. Pavers carry twenty-five-year to lifetime manufacturer warranties against cracking and fading. We've been in business eighteen years and plan to be here for every warranty claim.

Are you insured?
Yes — five million dollar liability coverage and WSIB compliant. We're happy to bring our certificate of insurance to the consultation.

Can you do pool decks?
Yes — pool deck installation is one of our specialties. We work in coordination with Aquaview Pools for projects involving new pool builds. We use slip-resistant coping units and structural geogrid to ensure the deck doesn't settle away from the pool shell over time.
