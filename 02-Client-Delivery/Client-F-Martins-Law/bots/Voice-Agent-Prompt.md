# Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need a lawyer to buy a house in Ontario?
A: You don't legally have to, but you'd be taking a real risk. The lawyer makes sure the title is clear, handles the money transfer, registers the deed, and protects you from fraud. For most people's biggest investment, it's worth having professional protection.

Q: What's the difference between a will and a power of attorney?
A: A will is for after you pass away - it says who gets your assets. A Power of Attorney is for while you're alive - it names someone to handle your finances or health decisions if you can't. You need both.

Q: How long does a real estate closing take?
A: Usually thirty to ninety days from when you sign the Agreement. You want to hire a lawyer as soon as you sign - there's a lot of coordination happening with banks and the other side's lawyer.

Q: Can you help if I'm buying from out of province?
A: Yes, we do that regularly. Everything can be handled by video call and email. You don't need to come to Ontario.

Q: What happens if I die without a will in Ontario?
A: The province decides who gets your assets, and it might not be what you'd want. It also costs more and takes longer to sort out. If you have kids, the court picks the guardian. Definitely better to have a will in place.

Q: How do I know if I need to probate an estate?
A: Generally you need probate if the person owned real estate alone, or if banks require it to release funds. Victor can assess that once he reviews what assets are involved.

Q: What is a closing adjustment?
A: It's making sure property taxes and utilities are split fairly between buyer and seller as of the closing date. If the seller prepaid taxes, the buyer reimburses them for the days they'll own the house.

Q: Do you handle title insurance?
A: Yes, we arrange that for every deal. It protects against title problems, fraud, and other issues. Most banks require it, and for cash purchases it's still a good idea.

Q: How quickly can you draft a will?
A: Usually one to two weeks, but we can turn it around in forty-eight hours if there's an emergency. Victor does a consultation first, then drafts it, then you sign with two witnesses.

Q: What do I bring to the initial consultation?
A: For real estate - your Agreement of Purchase and Sale, ID, and your realtor's info. For wills - a rough list of your assets, the names of who you'd want as beneficiaries, and who you'd want as executor. Don't worry if you don't have everything - Victor can work with what you've got.

Q: Do you handle commercial real estate?
A: Yes. Victor handles commercial real estate transactions and commercial leasing as well. The consultation will let him assess scope and give you an accurate quote.

Q: Are you the cheapest option in the GTA?
A: We're not the cheapest, and we're not the most expensive. We're competitive with GTA market rates. The difference is Victor handles every file personally - no paralegals, no handoffs, flat fees with no surprises.

Q: Can I do everything remotely?
