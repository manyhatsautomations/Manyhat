# Price & Budget Objections

"This is just too expensive!" I understand. When you say expensive, is that compared to another quote or more than you'd budgeted? Most clients find that doing it right the first time is actually the less costly decision — especially when the alternative is re-doing drainage issues or failed base work a few years down the road.

"That's more than I budgeted." That's fair — it catches people off guard sometimes. Which feels more risky: finding the funds to solve the problem for good, or going with a lower bid that skips the drainage prep and leaves you dealing with the same issue in three years?

"We don't have the money for this." Budget is always a key factor. If funding wasn't an issue, is this the solution you'd want? We can also look at phasing — tackle the retaining wall this season, add the patio next spring. We've helped a lot of clients spread costs that way.

"Can you give me a cheaper price?" Is getting the lowest price the main goal, or solving the problem so it stays solved? We can adjust scope to fit a budget, but it usually means a trade-off in results. Reuben's best placed to walk through those options with you in person.

"Can you just ballpark it over the phone?" I wish I could give you an accurate number without seeing the site, but every property in the Fraser Valley is different — access, soil conditions, drainage needs, and slope all affect pricing. What I can say is that most projects like what you're describing run somewhere between [relevant range]. Reuben can come out this week for a free estimate and give you exact numbers. Does Tuesday or Thursday work better?

"I don't have money for this right now." Budget timing is always a factor. Some clients use their home equity line of credit or a personal loan for larger projects — we don't offer in-house financing, but we're happy to work on phasing to spread costs over multiple seasons. When are you hoping to get this done?

"We are still price shopping." Makes sense. When you're comparing quotes, make sure they're including the same scope — drainage gravel, proper base prep, geotextile fabric, and a workmanship warranty. A lower bid that skips those steps costs more to fix later. We'll provide a detailed written quote so you can compare everything apples to apples.

"I've never done this before, that's more money than I've ever spent." I understand it feels like a big step. Which is more risky: making the investment to get the space you've always wanted, or staying where you are for another few years?

# Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long will my project take?
A: Depends on scope and weather. Small sod jobs might take one to two days. A complete backyard transformation can take two to three weeks. Reuben will give you a detailed timeline during your consultation. We don't rush quality work, but we also don't drag things out.

Q: Do I need a permit for a retaining wall?
A: In the Lower Mainland, retaining walls over four feet typically require a permit and engineering drawings. Vandyk handles the full permit application and engineering documentation as part of the service. Reuben will let you know during the consultation whether your project triggers that requirement. Never promise a permit will be approved — only that Vandyk will handle the application.

Q: What areas do you serve?
A: Vandyk is based in Langley and primarily serves the Fraser Valley and Lower Mainland — Surrey, Abbotsford, Aldergrove, Cloverdale, and surrounding communities within about 45 minutes of Langley. If you're unsure, just ask and we'll confirm with Reuben.

Q: Can you work with strata properties?
A: Absolutely. Vandyk works with stratas regularly and understands the approval process. They can provide all necessary documentation, insurance certificates, and attend strata meetings if needed to explain the project scope.

Q: When is the best time of year for a patio or retaining wall?
A: Spring through fall is ideal for hardscaping. But winter is actually the best time to plan and book your spring project — the schedule fills up fast. Sod and planting work best in spring and fall for optimal results.

Q: Do you offer financing?
A: Vandyk doesn't offer in-house financing. Many clients use their home equity line of credit or a personal loan for larger projects. Vandyk is also happy to work with you on phasing the project across multiple seasons to spread costs.

Q: Will a retaining wall stop my basement from flooding?
A: Proper drainage is built into every retaining wall project from day one — weep holes, gravel backfill, and drainage pipes to direct water away from the foundation. For more complex drainage, French drains or regrading may also be part of the solution. Reuben assesses the full drainage picture during the site visit.

Q: How do I maintain my new patio or lawn?
A: Hardscaping like patios and walls needs minimal maintenance — just occasional cleaning. New sod and plants come with care instructions. Vandyk also offers seasonal maintenance packages for landscapes they've installed.

Q: Are you insured?
A: Yes. Vandyk carries full liability insurance and WCB coverage. Certificates of insurance are available for strata or commercial projects.

Q: What happens if it rains during my installation?
A: Welcome to BC. Light rain doesn't stop work. Heavy rain might cause a short delay for safety and quality reasons. Reuben communicates any weather delays immediately and adjusts the schedule accordingly.

Q: Do you do weekly lawn mowing?
A: Vandyk specializes in installation and renovation — not ongoing maintenance. They do offer seasonal maintenance packages for landscapes they've installed. For regular mowing, a local lawn care company would be the right fit.

Q: Do you do tree removal?
A: Tree removal is a specialized trade — Vandyk doesn't handle that. A certified arborist would be the right contact.

Q: Do you do pool installation?
A: Pool construction is outside Vandyk's scope. If you're planning a pool and want the surrounding hardscape — patio, retaining walls, landscape area — that's exactly what Vandyk does. They can coordinate with your pool contractor to make sure everything aligns.

Q: Can I see examples of your work?
A: Absolutely. The best place is Instagram — at vandyk lawn and landscape — or the website at vandyk lawn landscape dot com. Reuben also brings portfolio examples to every site visit so you can see projects similar to yours.

Q: How do you handle payment?
A: Vandyk accepts e-transfers, cheques, or invoicing through their accounting software. Custom quotes are provided after the site visit — no deposits required at the booking stage.

Q: Do you work with realtors or investors?
A: Yes — Vandyk works with realtors and investors regularly on pre-sale improvements and rental property upgrades. They understand timelines and ROI focus. A consultation to talk through curb appeal improvements that offer the best return is a great starting point.
