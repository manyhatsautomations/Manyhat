# Camstruct-Specific Objections

"You're more expensive than the other quotes I got."
Totally fair — we're often not the cheapest bid, and we don't try to be. What I can tell you is our fixed-price contracts mean the quote you sign is the number you pay. No change-order surprises at the end. The contractors who come in low often make it up later. Which is more risky for a project of this size — the lower number up front, or knowing exactly what you're paying before a single wall comes down?

"I saw a YouTube video that said I could do this way cheaper."
YouTube makes everything look easier than it is — and I mean that genuinely, not as a dig. A full kitchen renovation involves eight to ten different trades, permit compliance, and about four hundred individual decisions. The savings on sweat equity often get eaten up by rework when sequencing goes wrong. We're happy to talk about a hybrid approach where you handle demolition or painting and we handle the technical work. Want to bring that up with Manny on the call?

"I've had a terrible experience with a contractor before."
That's exactly why we do things differently — and I'd rather hear what went wrong than gloss over it. What specifically happened? That actually helps me make sure we address it before you commit to anything with us.

"I'm worried it'll take forever."
That's a fair concern. Our on-time completion rate is 98%. Industry average is 63%. We hit our deadlines by keeping trades on exclusive retainer instead of scrambling for availability, and using project management software to sequence the work properly. We've delivered projects two months ahead of schedule. Manny can walk you through how we'd approach your timeline specifically.

"I don't want to live in a construction zone for a year."
Completely valid. For partial renovations, we create temporary setups and work in phases so you can stay in the house. For full gut renovations or custom builds, we plan realistic displacement timelines upfront so there are no surprises. Manny has helped dozens of families manage this — worth bringing up on the call.

"How do I know you won't go over budget?"
We use fixed-price contracts with detailed line-item breakdowns. Guaranteed maximum price. If anything changes, it goes through a formal change order process — priced before work starts. Nothing surprises you mid-project. That's one of the main reasons people come to us after getting burned elsewhere.

"I need to talk to my spouse or partner first."
Makes total sense. Is there any information I can have ready for you to share with them? And would it help to get them on the call with Manny too — he's good at walking both of you through the process at the same time.

"I'm not sure I can afford this."
Budget is exactly what a call with Manny is for. He's very direct about what's realistic at different price points and what trade-offs look like. Sometimes the scope gets adjusted, sometimes financing options open things up. Would it help to at least know what's possible before deciding?

"Can you guarantee this will work?"
We can cover guarantees on our call — five-year craftsmanship warranty, fixed-price contracts, 98% on-time track record. Tell me — what's behind the question? I want to understand what you need to feel secure before moving forward.
