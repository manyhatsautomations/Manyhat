# TURF-SPECIFIC OBJECTIONS

"How much does it cost? / What's the price per square foot?"
Prices range from $8 to $25 per sq ft fully installed, depending on the turf type, site prep,
and access. A typical residential landscape project runs $12–18 per sq ft. For example, a
500 sq ft backyard is usually $6,500–$16,000.

To give you a number that actually means something, Taylor needs to measure the space and
check access — narrow gates add labor, and drainage prep varies by site. The site visit is
free and typically runs 30–60 minutes. Worth doing before you get locked into a budget.

"It's too expensive."
Turf is more upfront than resodding, no question. But the math is interesting. Natural grass
in BC — watering, mowing, reseeding, fertilizing — runs most homeowners $1,500–$2,000 a year
to maintain. Our turf costs about $100 a year to maintain. Most clients break even in 5–7
years. After that, the lawn is basically paying you back.

Which feels riskier to you: the upfront investment to fix the problem for good, or spending
that money every year on a lawn that still frustrates you?

"It looks fake / plastic."
That was true five years ago. Today we use variable blade technology — four blade colors
mixed at irregular heights to mimic natural growth. You can walk past it and genuinely not
know. I can show you installed examples in your area, or Taylor can bring samples to your
door. It's the kind of thing you have to see and touch to believe.

"It gets too hot for my kids / dogs."
Valid concern with older turf. Our premium products use HydroChill infill — an evaporative
cooling system that drops surface temperature by 30–50 degrees compared to standard rubber
infill. On really hot days, a two-minute rinse activates the cooling effect. It runs cooler
than your concrete patio.

"What about drainage? I already have flooding issues."
Actually, artificial turf drains significantly better than real grass. Natural soil compacts
over time and puddles. Our installations include a 3–4 inch crushed aggregate base with a
perforated backing that handles 60 to 300 inches of water per hour. Jay Tuck had a backyard
that flooded every winter. After his install, no flooding issues — and he's running three
large dogs on it.

"Is it safe for pets?"
100%. Our pet turf uses flow-through backing and antimicrobial infill (Microban technology).
It drains 300+ inches of rain per hour and prevents odor at the source. No mud, no brown
spots, no smell. Jay Tuck's been running three large dogs on his for over a year — still
looks great.

"Is it safe for kids?"
Our playground turf is designed specifically for safety — impact attenuation, shock pads,
non-abrasive surface. No mud, no splinters, no maintenance chemicals. It's cleaner and
