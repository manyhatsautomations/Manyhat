"How much does it cost? What's the price per square foot?"
Prices range from eight to twenty-five dollars per square foot fully installed, depending on
the turf type, site prep, and access. A typical residential landscape project runs twelve to
eighteen dollars per square foot. A five hundred square foot backyard is usually six thousand

To give you a number that actually means something, Taylor needs to measure the space and check
access — narrow gates add labor, and drainage prep varies by site. The site visit is free and
typically runs 30 to 60 minutes. Worth doing before you get locked into a budget.

"It's too expensive."
Turf is more upfront than resodding, no question. But the math is interesting. Natural grass
in BC — watering, mowing, reseeding, fertilizing — runs most homeowners one thousand five
hundred to two thousand dollars a year to maintain. Our turf costs about one hundred dollars
a year to maintain. Most clients break even in five to seven years. After that, the lawn is
basically paying you back.

Which feels riskier to you: the upfront investment to fix the problem for good, or spending
that money every year on a lawn that still frustrates you?

"It looks fake or plastic."
That was true five years ago. Today we use variable blade technology — four blade colors mixed
at irregular heights to mimic natural growth. You can walk past it and genuinely not know.
Taylor can bring samples to your door at the site visit. It's the kind of thing you have to
see and touch to believe.

"It gets too hot for my kids or dogs."
Valid concern with older turf. Our premium products use HydroChill infill — an evaporative
cooling system that drops surface temperature by 30 to 50 degrees compared to standard rubber
infill. On really hot days, a two-minute rinse activates the cooling effect. It runs cooler
than your concrete patio.

"What about drainage? I already have flooding issues."
Actually, artificial turf drains significantly better than real grass. Natural soil compacts
over time and puddles. Our installations include a three to four inch crushed aggregate base
with a perforated backing that handles 60 to 300 inches of water per hour. Jay Tuck had a
backyard that flooded every winter. After his install, no flooding issues — and he's running
three large dogs on it.

"Is it safe for pets?"
One hundred percent. Our pet turf uses flow-through backing and antimicrobial infill. It drains
over three hundred inches of rain per hour and prevents odor at the source. No mud, no brown
spots, no smell. Jay Tuck's been running three large dogs on his for over a year — still looks
great.

"Is it safe for kids?"
Our playground turf is designed specifically for safety — impact attenuation, shock pads,
non-abrasive surface. No mud, no splinters, no maintenance chemicals. It's cleaner and softer
than most natural grass setups.

"I've heard it's toxic or has lead or PFAS."
We only use certified lead-free, non-toxic turf. The concerns you've read about apply to old
crumb rubber infill from recycled tires — common in older sports fields, not what we install.
We use acrylic-coated sand or Zeolite — both safe for kids and pets and actually antimicrobial.

"Will my HOA allow it?"
Many HOAs in BC have updated their rules, especially around water conservation. We have an HOA

Of course. Usually when someone needs to think it over, it's either the budget or the timing.
